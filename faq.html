Expert Insights & FAQs

Hiring for AI and Emerging Tech Roles

What skills should I look for in an AI product manager?

Look beyond traditional product management experience. You need someone who understands machine learning fundamentals, can communicate with data scientists, and has shipped products using AI. They should grasp both the technical capabilities and limitations of AI systems.

How do we hire for roles that didn't exist five years ago?

The answer is you focus on foundational skills and learning ability over direct experience. Someone with strong systems thinking, a track record of picking up new technologies quickly, and genuine curiosity matters more than a perfect resume match. Look for folks who've successfully pivoted in their careers.

Why do traditional recruiting methods fail for AI roles?

Because the talent pool is incredibly small and passive. Most skilled AI professionals aren't actively job hunting. You need to build relationships, offer interesting technical challenges, and often partner with specialized recruiters who understand the space.

What's the difference between an AI engineer and an AI researcher?

Engineers focus on productizing and scaling AI systems for real-world use. Researchers push the boundaries of what's possible. For most companies, you want engineers, but you might need researchers if you're doing cutting-edge development.

Robotics and Advanced Manufacturing Recruitment

How do you evaluate a robotics engineer's portfolio versus their resume?

The portfolio matters far more. Look at actual projects they've built, automation systems they've designed, code they've written. A resume tells you what they claim to have done, but a portfolio proves technical depth and problem-solving ability.

What red flags should manufacturers look for when hiring automation specialists?

Watch out for folks who can't explain their past projects clearly. If they're vague about what they actually built, that's telling. Also be wary of candidates who haven't kept up with modern tools and frameworks. The field moves fast and complacency is dangerous.

Why is it hard to find talent in industrial automation?

It's niche. Most engineers go into software or consumer tech because it feels sexier and pays better initially. Manufacturing automation requires a specific blend of mechanical engineering, electrical knowledge, and programming skills that's rare. Plus, much of the expertise sits with older engineers nearing retirement.

What certifications matter for manufacturing roles?

It depends on the role. For some positions, relevant certifications in specific control systems or machinery matter. But honestly, proven experience and the ability to troubleshoot real systems often trumps certifications. Ask candidates about their hands-on experience more than their certificates.

Building High-Intent Teams in Fintech and Insurtech

What's the typical hiring timeline for AI infrastructure roles?

Expect six to nine months for senior positions. These candidates are passive, there's heavy competition, and the vetting process is rigorous because the stakes are high. If you're expecting a quick hire, you're in for disappointment.

How do salary expectations compare across AI, robotics, and fintech?

AI roles pay a premium, often 20 to 40 percent above traditional software engineering. Robotics and advanced manufacturing tend to pay slightly less but are rising. Fintech and insurtech compete aggressively on compensation. You need realistic budgets or you won't attract the talent.

What does onboarding look like for specialized technical roles?

These folks need mentorship from day one, access to cutting-edge tools and resources, and clear technical challenges. Generic corporate onboarding won't cut it. They'll leave if they feel like they're not growing or working on interesting problems.

How do we compete with big tech companies for talent?

You likely can't match their salaries, so focus on autonomy, meaningful work, and impact. Engineers in AI and robotics often care deeply about the application of their skills. A fintech startup solving real problems can be more attractive than a megacorp.

Resume and Interview Best Practices for Tech-Forward Roles

What should a strong resume look like for an AI role?

It should highlight specific projects with measurable impact. Don't just list skills. Show what they built, what problem it solved, and what the results were. Numbers matter. "Improved model accuracy by 15 percent" beats "experienced with machine learning."

How do you interview a robotics engineer effectively?

Ask them to walk through a project they built from start to finish. Ask why they made specific technical choices. Dig into how they'd approach a problem they've never solved before. You want to see their thinking process, not just their knowledge.

What questions separate truly skilled candidates from resume builders?

Ask them to explain a failure and what they learned. Ask them to describe a technical decision they'd make differently if they could go back. Ask how they stay current in a rapidly evolving field. Their honesty and self-awareness matter as much as their skills.

How do we assess cultural fit without generic team values?

In specialized fields, cultural fit means intellectual compatibility and aligned work standards. Someone who's passionate about their craft, collaborative with specialists from other disciplines, and genuinely interested in the problem space will succeed. That matters more than whether they like ping pong.
