Technical Intelligence & FAQ

Hiring for AI & Emerging Tech

What skills should I look for in an AI Product Manager?

Look beyond traditional PM experience. You need someone who understands machine learning fundamentals, can communicate with data scientists, and has shipped products using AI. They must grasp both the technical capabilities and the inherent limitations of AI systems.

How do we hire for roles that didn't exist five years ago?

Focus on foundational skills and learning velocity over direct experience. Someone with strong systems thinking and a track record of picking up new technologies quickly matters more than a "perfect" resume match. Look for successful career pivots.

Why do traditional recruiting methods fail for AI roles?

The talent pool is incredibly small and 85% passive. Most skilled AI professionals aren't actively job hunting. You must offer interesting technical challenges and partner with specialized recruiters who actually speak the language.

What's the difference between an AI Engineer and an AI Researcher?

Engineers focus on productizing and scaling AI systems for real-world use. Researchers push the boundaries of what's possible. Most companies need Engineers to drive ROI, while cutting-edge R&D firms require Researchers.

Robotics & Advanced Manufacturing

How do you evaluate a Robotics Engineer's portfolio?

The portfolio matters far more than the resume. Look at actual systems they've built, automation cells they've designed, and code they've written. A portfolio proves technical depth and real-world problem-solving ability.

What red flags should manufacturers look for in automation specialists?

Watch for vagueness. If a candidate can't explain the "why" behind their build or hasn't kept up with modern frameworks, it’s a red flag. In this field, complacency is dangerous.

Why is it so hard to find Industrial Automation talent?

It's a rare blend of mechanical engineering, electrical knowledge, and programming. Most engineers gravitate toward pure software; manufacturing requires a "hands-on" specialist, and much of that expertise is currently nearing retirement.

Fintech, Insurtech & Team Strategy

What is the typical hiring timeline for AI Infrastructure roles?

Expect 6 to 9 months for senior positions. These candidates are passive and competition is fierce. If you're expecting a "quick hire" for a high-stakes infrastructure role, you're in for disappointment.

How do salary expectations compare across these sectors?

AI roles pay a premium—often 20% to 40% above traditional software engineering. Robotics is rising, while Fintech and Insurtech compete aggressively on total compensation. You need a realistic budget to attract preeminent talent.

How do we compete with Big Tech for this talent?

You likely can't match their cash, so focus on autonomy, meaningful work, and impact. Engineers in AI and Robotics care deeply about the *application* of their skills. A mission-driven startup is often more attractive than a megacorp.

Resume & Interview Best Practices

What questions separate skilled candidates from "resume builders"?

Ask them to explain a failure and what they learned. Ask them to describe a technical decision they'd make differently today. Honesty and self-awareness in technical pivots matter as much as the skill itself.

How do we assess cultural fit in specialized fields?

Cultural fit means intellectual compatibility and aligned work standards. Someone passionate about their craft and collaborative with specialists from other disciplines will succeed. This matters more than generic "team values."
